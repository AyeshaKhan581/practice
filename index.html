Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Founder of Pakistan




Early Life


Muhammad Ali Jinnah was born December 25, 1876. In Karachi, British India (now Pakistan). He was the eldest of seven children in a mercantile family. Jinnah was educated at the Bombay University and Lincoln's Inn in London, where he trained as a barrister.


Political Career


Jinnah was a prominent leader of the All-India Muslim League and played a pivotal role in the creation of Pakistan. His vision and leadership were instrumental in the partition of India and establishment of Pakistan as a separate Nation in 1947.


Legacy


Jinnah served as Pakistan's first Governor General until his death on September 11, 1948. He is revered in Pakistan as the 'Father of the Nation' and his birthday celebrated as a national holiday.
